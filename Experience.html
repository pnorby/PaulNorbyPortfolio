Experience

My first job as a developer was a very interesting experience. I was working for a newer company called MC Integration Solutions. Working there part-time while in school was a nice transition into the developer work force. I learned a lot about how different medical providers share data, and some gaps that currently exist in the system. Coding solutions for these gaps provided a creative atmosphere utilizing several skills including JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. I enjoyed working at MC, but wanted experience working with a larger organization which brought me to WPS Health Solutions.

As a .NET Software Developer Intern WPS Health Solutions I am getting the experience that I need to be a successful back-end developer. I am working with larger applications than what I was exposed to in school which has been challenging but doable. Working with code that is not my own has also been a strange transition, as I have encountered a few different styles of thinking and organization within the code. Following an established Software Development Life Cycle has also been a great experience. Working for a larger organization like WPS, you need to work with different business units to achieve common goals. The exposure to this team atmosphere is a change from the mostly isolated classroom assignments and has been a nice way to pave the way from student to professional.
